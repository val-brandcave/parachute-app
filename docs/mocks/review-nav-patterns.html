OPTION 1 · recommended

Track switch on the identity row

Demote the track from its own band onto the meta line (right-aligned segmented switch). Only the stage tabs remain as a band. When a loan has one track, the switch simply doesn't render — no orphan. Stages get their vertical space back.

OPTION 1b · densest

One row: stages left, track switch right

Collapse both dimensions onto a single band — stages on the left, track switch on the right edge. Maximum vertical savings. The one awkward moment: when Administrative (no stages) is selected, the left side is empty and the row just holds the switch.

OPTION 2

Track switcher in the breadcrumb

Treat the track as scope — a switcher built into the breadcrumb (click it). Semantically cleanest and removes the most chrome, but "a second deliverable exists" is hidden behind a caret, so it needs a count badge to stay discoverable. Probably too quiet for a pending attestation.

OPTION 3

Stages as a left sub-rail

Track stays a top switch; stages move to a vertical rail beside the content (Stripe / Vercel / Linear-settings pattern). Costs zero vertical space and scales — but it steals horizontal width that Builder's 3-pane tool and the wide Workbook doc need, and adds a second left edge next to the app sidebar.

Recommendation

Option 1 (track switch on the identity row) + a touch of Option 4 (status on the stage tabs). It answers all three concerns — kills the redundant band, keeps both tracks visible, degrades cleanly to one track — while preserving the locked, Cody-approved 3-stage underline sub-nav.
